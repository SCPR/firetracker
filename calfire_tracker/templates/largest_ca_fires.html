About the GIS data: The fire perimeters that accompany some of these fires are approximate, and are from the Geospatial Multi-Agency Coordination Group, or GeoMAC.


Each shapefile contained thousands of attributes, many of them redundant or slightly different versions of each fire. That's because, as the agencies took new measurements each day, they simply added new boundaries to the same file. In addition, there appear to be multiple methods for calculating the boundaries: Infrared heat mapping versus the Fire Incident Mapping Tool or on-the-ground GPS measurements, etc.).


Without a lot more research time, it's difficult to say which set we ought to consider as the authoritative or best count. No matter which one you choose, it appeared to be different from CalFire's acreage count in almost every case.
